About the Observatory

The Brazilian Extremist Content Hashing Observatory (BR-ECHO) is an academic, non-commercial research project developed at the University of São Paulo (USP) in collaboration with King’s College London (KCL).

Access Restriction: This application is a private research tool. Access to the BR-ECHO interface and its data is strictly restricted to the internal research team and authorized academic collaborators. It is not available for public or commercial use.

Managed by researcher Ricardo Cabral Penteado, BR-ECHO utilizes advanced computational linguistics and hashing techniques to monitor and analyze public discourse trends for scholarly purposes.

This initiative contributes to the global understanding of digital platform governance and online safety by utilizing the TikTok Research API under strict ethical guidelines.
